Why we built this

A bridge built
from hours.

We started Samay Setu because the most valuable thing a neighbourhood has — its people's time — was the one thing no one had a fair way to share.

Cities are full of people standing on opposite banks of the same river. On one side, a retired teacher with afternoons to spare. On the other, a working parent who can't find an hour for their child's maths. They live streets apart and never meet — because there was no bridge between what one could give and what the other needed.

Money was supposed to be that bridge. But money decides that some hours are worth more than others, and quietly tells too many people their time isn't worth much at all. We didn't believe that.

So we built an exchange with one rule money could never follow: an hour is an hour. The surgeon and the student, the coder and the cook — inside the circle, their time carries exactly the same weight.

We're starting where we live: a few streets of North Kolkata, twenty neighbours, hand-verified one at a time. Small enough that trust is real. Honest enough that it lasts.

What we hold to

Three things we
won't compromise.

i.

Equal by design

The platform structurally refuses to price one person's hour above another's. Equality isn't a slogan here — it's the math.

ii.

Small on purpose

We grow in waves, not floods. A circle people can actually know is worth more than a crowd they can't trust.

iii.

Human first

No ads, no selling your data, no engagement traps. The product is the relationship between neighbours — nothing else.

North Kolkata — where the lanes still remember everyone's name.

Why North Kolkata

We started where
community already lived.

North Kolkata's paras have shared, lent, and looked after one another for generations. The instinct to help a neighbour is already here — it just lost its bridge somewhere along the way.

We're not importing a Silicon Valley idea. We're giving an old, local habit a fair and modern shape — and then we'll let the next neighbourhood decide if it wants one too.
