Four ways to sound
like Samay Setu.

One typeface can't carry a brand — a system can. Each option below assigns a font to three jobs and shows them on real Samay Setu copy, so you can judge them in context, not in isolation. Pick the system whose voice fits; I'll then apply it across every page and remove the generic touches in one pass.

Role 1 — Display
Headlines & numbers
Hero lines, section titles, the wordmark, big figures. Carries the personality.
Role 2 — Text
Body & interface
Ledes, paragraphs, UI labels, buttons. Must stay calm and readable at length.
Role 3 — Label
Kickers & meta
Eyebrows, tags, countdowns, data. A mono or technical accent for texture.

My recommendation

Option A — The Press, with one tweak.

It keeps the editorial soul you already have but swaps the slightly generic pairing for fonts with real character: Fraunces (display) has the old-style warmth Cormorant aimed at, with more personality and better numerals; Newsreader reads beautifully at length; Spline Sans Mono adds a modern technical accent. If you want to feel less old-world and more "now," go Option B (The Grotesque). If place/identity matters most, Option D gives you true bilingual unity. Tell me which one (or mix roles across options) and I'll roll it through every page — and in the same pass strip the AI-slop tells: decorative emoji in the UI, the ✦ marquee filler, single-letter avatars everywhere, over-centered symmetry, and the always-on fake "live" tickers.
